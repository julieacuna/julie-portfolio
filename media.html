Media and Creativity

Creative Communication

Media and creativity are important parts of how I understand communication. I enjoy projects that combine writing, visuals, music, design, and storytelling because they can make ideas feel more personal and memorable.

As a public relations student, I am interested in how media can shape the way people see brands, communities, artists, events, and social issues. A strong message is not only about the words being used. It is also about the tone, visuals, audience, and purpose behind the message.

Photography and Video

Photography and video have helped me think more carefully about visual storytelling. A single image can capture emotion, movement, personality, or atmosphere. Video can add another layer by showing sound, timing, and action.

My experience with student media helped me become more aware of composition, detail, and the importance of documenting real moments. I enjoy media work because it allows me to be both observant and creative.

Design and Storytelling

I also enjoy graphic design and digital creativity. Design helps organize information and create a mood for the viewer. Color, spacing, fonts, images, and layout all affect how a message feels.

In the future, I hope to continue building creative skills that connect public relations, psychology, media, and visual communication.

Creative Skills

  • Video storytelling
  • Graphic design
  • Social media content
  • Public relations strategy
  • Audience awareness
